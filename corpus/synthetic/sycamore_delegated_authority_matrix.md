## 1. Purpose

1.1 This Schedule sets out the underwriting authorities delegated by the Active Underwriter of Syndicate 4271 to underwriters, deputies, and external parties acting under Binding Authority or other delegated arrangement.

1.2 This Schedule is approved annually by the Active Underwriter and reviewed at least quarterly. Amendments between scheduled reviews require sign-off by the Active Underwriter and the Chief Underwriting Officer; amendments affecting Coverholder authority require notification to Lloyd's per the Coverholder Approval and Performance Management requirements.

1.3 Authority granted under this Schedule is subject at all times to the Risk Appetite Statement (SYC-RAS-2026-01) and the class-specific Underwriting Guidelines.

## 2. Authority hierarchy

2.1 Underwriting authority cascades through four levels:

| Level | Role | Indicative authority range |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Active Underwriter | Unlimited within RAS limits; final authority on all risks |
| 2 | Class Underwriter | Up to authority specified in Section 4 |
| 3 | Deputy / Assistant Underwriter | Up to 50% of Class Underwriter limit; referral required above |
| 4 | Coverholder (delegated authority) | As specified in the individual Binding Authority agreement |

2.2 No underwriter shall write a risk in excess of their stated authority. Risks above an underwriter's limit must be referred to the next level up.

## 3. Definitions

"Class Underwriter" means the senior underwriter responsible for a Class of Business, as set out in Section 4.

"Coverholder" means a Lloyd's-approved third party authorised under a written Binding Authority to underwrite specified risks on behalf of the Syndicate.

"Binding Authority" means an agreement, in the standard Lloyd's Market Association ("LMA") form or an equivalent, granting a Coverholder delegated underwriting authority subject to defined limits and reporting requirements.

"Open Market" business means risks placed directly by brokers without delegated underwriting, where each risk is individually underwritten by the Syndicate's box.

"Slip" means the contract document for an Open Market risk, prepared by the placing broker and signed by the underwriter.

## 5. Coverholder requirements

5.1 All Coverholders operating under a Binding Authority must hold valid Lloyd's Coverholder approval and the Syndicate's internal Coverholder due diligence sign-off, refreshed annually.

5.2 Each Binding Authority must specify, at minimum:

(a) The class(es) of business and territorial scope;
(b) The per-risk and aggregate authority limits;
(c) Excluded risks;
(d) Premium rating method and reference rates;
(e) Reporting requirements (typically monthly bordereaux);
(f) Audit rights and frequency;
(g) Termination provisions.

5.3 Coverholders are reviewed quarterly by the Delegated Authority Committee. Material performance deviations — loss ratio above plan by more than 10 percentage points; bordereaux late by more than 30 days; conduct complaints — trigger formal Performance Review and may result in suspension or termination of authority.

## 6. Escalation

6.1 Risks falling outside a delegated authority must be referred upward. Authority above £25m single-risk GWP, irrespective of class, rests solely with the Active Underwriter.

6.2 Authority above the Active Underwriter's RAS limits requires Board approval per Section 8 of the Risk Appetite Statement.

## 7. Documentation requirements

7.1 Every risk bound under any authority must be documented in the Syndicate's underwriting system with:

(a) The Slip or equivalent contract document;
(b) Reference to the authority level under which it was bound;
(c) For Coverholder business, reference to the relevant Binding Authority agreement.

7.2 Authority breaches identified through internal monitoring, audit, or external review are recorded in the Underwriting Exception Log and reported to the Risk Committee.
