## 1. Purpose and scope

1.1 This policy ("the Policy") sets out Sycamore Re's underwriting position on risks materially associated with thermal coal. It implements the climate and ESG commitments made in the Risk Appetite Statement (SYC-RAS-2026-01, Section 6) and gives effect to the Syndicate's alignment with the Lloyd's Sustainable Marketplace Initiative.

1.2 The Policy applies to all Open Market and Binding Authority business written across all classes of the Syndicate. It binds the Active Underwriter, all underwriters operating under delegated authority, and all Coverholders.

1.3 This Policy is not the entirety of Sycamore Re's climate and ESG underwriting framework. It is supplemented by parallel policies on oil sands, Arctic energy, and other carbon-intensive sectors, maintained in the ESG Underwriting Guidelines.

## 2. Definitions

"Thermal Coal" means coal extracted, transported, or burned for energy generation, excluding metallurgical coal used in steel production.

"Thermal Coal Operation" means a mine, port, terminal, transport infrastructure, or power generation facility whose primary purpose is the extraction, handling, or use of Thermal Coal for energy generation.

"New Thermal Coal Operation" means a Thermal Coal Operation for which final investment decision was taken on or after 1 January 2020.

"Transition Plan" means a published, third-party-verifiable plan committing to the closure or repurposing of Thermal Coal Operations on a timeline consistent with the Paris Agreement, typically a phase-out by 2030 for OECD jurisdictions and by 2040 globally.

## 3. Sycamore Re's position

3.1 Sycamore Re will not knowingly underwrite, in any class of business, any New Thermal Coal Operation. This restriction applies to direct insurance and reinsurance of property, liability, business interruption, marine cargo, construction, and any other coverage attaching to such an operation.

3.2 Existing Thermal Coal Operations — those for which final investment decision predates 1 January 2020 — remain insurable subject to the conditions set out in Section 4.

3.3 This Policy does not restrict the underwriting of risks that are merely connected with the broader energy sector. Reinsurance of a diversified utility, for example, is not excluded by this Policy, but specific underwriting of a thermal coal asset within that utility's portfolio is.

## 4. Conditions for existing Thermal Coal Operations

4.1 Existing Thermal Coal Operations may be underwritten subject to all of the following conditions being satisfied and documented:

(a) The operator publishes a Transition Plan meeting the definition in Section 2;

(b) The operator's most recent annual sustainability or climate-related disclosure does not identify material non-compliance with their Transition Plan;

(c) The risk is not a single-location stand-alone Thermal Coal Operation contributing more than 10% of the operator's enterprise carbon footprint;

(d) Underwriter sign-off from the Class Underwriter is obtained on each risk and recorded in the underwriting file.

4.2 Where any of conditions 4.1(a) through 4.1(c) is not satisfied, the risk is restricted regardless of class limit or pricing adequacy. The decision sits with the Active Underwriter, with documented rationale, in consultation with the ESG Committee.

## 5. Decision framework

5.1 Underwriters must apply the following framework to any risk that includes Thermal Coal exposure:

**Question 1.** Is the risk a New Thermal Coal Operation per Section 2? → If yes: **DECLINE**.

**Question 2.** Is the risk an Existing Thermal Coal Operation? → If yes: proceed to Question 3.

**Question 3.** Does the operator have a published Transition Plan per Section 2? → If no: refer to Section 4.2 (Active Underwriter / ESG Committee).

**Question 4.** Does the operator's most recent disclosure show material non-compliance with the Transition Plan? → If yes: refer to Section 4.2.

**Question 5.** Does the risk concentrate more than 10% of the operator's enterprise carbon footprint in a single Thermal Coal Operation? → If yes: refer to Section 4.2.

**Question 6.** Has Class Underwriter sign-off been obtained and documented? → If no: obtain before binding.

→ **Bind** subject to standard underwriting and pricing.

5.2 Where an underwriter is unable to determine the answer to any of Questions 1–5 from the information available, the risk is referred upward.

## 6. Coverholder application

6.1 The restriction in Section 3.1 applies in full to business bound under Binding Authority. Each Binding Authority covering classes that may include Thermal Coal exposure must include an explicit Thermal Coal exclusion clause referencing this Policy.

6.2 Coverholder bordereaux are screened monthly for Thermal Coal exposure. Identified exposures are reviewed by the ESG Committee at its next scheduled meeting.

## 7. Documentation and reporting

7.1 Risks involving Thermal Coal exposure (whether bound or declined) are tagged in the underwriting system with the "TCOAL" exposure marker.

7.2 The Head of ESG and Sustainability reports quarterly to the ESG Committee on:

(a) Risks bound under Section 4 (existing operations meeting conditions);
(b) Risks declined under Section 3.1 (new operations);
(c) Risks referred under Section 4.2 (operator without Transition Plan or non-compliant);
(d) Coverholder exposures identified during bordereaux screening.

7.3 Material Thermal Coal exposures are disclosed in the Syndicate's annual sustainability statement.
