## 1. Purpose and scope

1.1 This Risk Appetite Statement ("RAS") sets out the risk appetite of Syndicate 4271 ("the Syndicate") for the 2026 Year of Account ("2026 YoA"). It is approved annually by the board of Sycamore Underwriting Limited ("the Managing Agent" or "SUL") and forms part of the Syndicate's overall risk management framework.

1.2 This document applies to all underwriting and risk-taking activity conducted on behalf of the Syndicate by the Managing Agent, its employees, and any party operating under delegated authority. It is to be read alongside:

- The Syndicate Business Forecast ("SBF") for 2026 YoA;
- The Syndicate's Solvency II Internal Model capital submission to Lloyd's;
- The Delegated Underwriting Authority Schedule (SYC-UAS-2026-01); and
- Class-specific Underwriting Guidelines as maintained by the Active Underwriter.

1.3 In the event of conflict between this RAS and any subordinate document, this RAS prevails.

## 2. Definitions

"Risk Capacity" means the maximum aggregate risk the Syndicate could absorb before its solvency or operational continuity would be compromised, expressed against the Syndicate's regulatory capital requirement.

"Risk Appetite" means the level and type of risk the Managing Agent is willing to accept on behalf of Members in pursuit of the Syndicate's business objectives.

"Risk Tolerance" means the acceptable variation around Risk Appetite at which monitoring and escalation thresholds are set.

"Risk Limit" means a specific quantitative ceiling, beyond which the Syndicate will not underwrite or hold a position without escalation per Section 8.

"Members" means the corporate and individual capital providers participating on the 2026 YoA of Syndicate 4271.

## 3. Risk appetite philosophy

3.1 Sycamore Re underwrites at Lloyd's to generate sustainable returns for Members across the underwriting cycle. The Syndicate accepts insurance and reinsurance risk as its primary risk-taking activity and seeks to limit other risk categories — market, credit, operational, and group — to the extent consistent with the prudent management of an active underwriting venture.

3.2 The Managing Agent operates the Syndicate within a two-line framework:

(a) **Hard limits**, expressed as quantitative ceilings against capital or capacity, breach of which is treated as a regulatory matter requiring immediate escalation to the Board and notification to Lloyd's Performance Management Directorate ("PMD") where required by Lloyd's Minimum Standards.

(b) **Soft preferences**, expressed as qualitative principles or target ranges, deviation from which is permissible with documented rationale and Active Underwriter approval.

3.3 The Syndicate's appetite is calibrated to maintain a 1-in-200-year solvency position consistent with Solvency II requirements and to remain within Lloyd's Realistic Disaster Scenario ("RDS") tolerances at all times.

## 4. Quantitative risk appetite

4.1 The Syndicate's 2026 YoA stamp capacity is £350 million. Risk appetite is expressed against capacity except where stated otherwise.

| Risk category | Appetite metric | 2026 limit |
|---|---|---|
| Aggregate net underwriting exposure | Net PML, 1-in-200, all perils | ≤ 35% of capacity |
| Single risk gross written premium | GWP, single risk | ≤ £25 million |
| Single risk net retention | Net retention after RI | ≤ £8 million |
| Property catastrophe net AEP | 1-in-100 AEP net of RI | ≤ £45 million |
| North Atlantic windstorm net OEP | 1-in-250 OEP net of RI | ≤ £55 million |
| California earthquake net OEP | 1-in-250 OEP net of RI | ≤ £35 million |
| Counterparty credit (reinsurers) | Total exposure to single counterparty | ≤ £40 million |
| Investment portfolio interest rate risk | DV01 of bond portfolio | ≤ £0.8 million |
| Liquidity coverage | Cash + near-cash / 12-month claims | ≥ 1.5× |
| Operational risk | Annual operational losses | ≤ £2 million |

4.2 Limits are reviewed quarterly by the Risk Committee. Mid-year increases require Board approval and notification to Lloyd's PMD where the change is material.

## 5. Qualitative principles

5.1 The Syndicate writes only business classes for which it has Active Underwriter expertise and demonstrated track record. New classes require Board approval following a documented business case and capital impact assessment.

5.2 The Syndicate does not write business outside the territorial scope agreed in its annual SBF without prior Active Underwriter approval and, where the territorial expansion is material, Board ratification.

5.3 Cession arrangements (reinsurance protections purchased by the Syndicate) are concentrated among Lloyd's-rated and S&P A- or better reinsurers. The Reinsurance Security Committee maintains the approved counterparty list.

5.4 The Syndicate maintains diversification across the four primary classes — Property D&F, Energy, Specialty Treaty, and Marine & Cargo — at the levels documented in the SBF, with deviation tolerances of ±15% by class.

## 6. Climate and ESG risk appetite

6.1 The Syndicate is committed to the Lloyd's Sustainable Marketplace Initiative and aligns its underwriting and investment decisions with Lloyd's market-level decarbonisation commitments.

6.2 The Syndicate maintains explicit underwriting restrictions on thermal coal, oil sands, and Arctic energy exploration as set out in the Thermal Coal Underwriting Policy (SYC-ESG-COAL-2026) and the broader ESG Underwriting Guidelines.

6.3 The Syndicate has set an internal trajectory to reduce the carbon intensity of its underwritten portfolio in line with Lloyd's market guidance, measured annually. The trajectory and progress against it are reported to the Board's ESG Committee and disclosed in the Syndicate's annual sustainability statement.

6.4 Physical and transition climate risks are explicitly considered in the Syndicate's catastrophe modelling assumptions, scenario testing, and reserving. The Risk Committee oversees the climate-specific stress tests required by PRA SS5/25.

## 7. Operational and conduct risk

7.1 The Syndicate's operational resilience approach is set out in the Operational Resilience Framework, which establishes Important Business Services, Impact Tolerances, and tested recovery scenarios consistent with PRA SS1/21.

7.2 The Syndicate has no appetite for conduct breaches, regulatory censure, or harm to policyholders. Reportable conduct events trigger immediate escalation per Section 8.

## 8. Escalation and breach

8.1 Any breach or anticipated breach of a hard limit in Section 4 must be:

(a) Reported by the Active Underwriter to the Chief Risk Officer within one business day of identification;

(b) Reviewed by the Risk Committee at its next meeting, or sooner where the breach is material;

(c) Reported to Lloyd's PMD per Lloyd's Minimum Standards where the breach is reportable.

8.2 Deviation from a qualitative principle in Section 5 is recorded in the Underwriting Exception Log. Cumulative qualitative deviations are reviewed quarterly by the Risk Committee, which may convert recurrent deviations into a documented amendment to this RAS.

8.3 The Active Underwriter is responsible for ensuring underwriting activity remains within the limits and principles set out in this RAS.

## 9. Governance and review

9.1 This RAS is reviewed annually by the Risk Committee ahead of the annual Board approval cycle. The Chief Risk Officer is the document owner.

9.2 Material amendments outside the annual review cycle require Board approval.

9.3 The Internal Audit function tests adherence to this RAS as part of its annual audit plan.
